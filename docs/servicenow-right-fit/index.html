Is ServiceNow the best option for my team?

There is definitive way to answer this question - but a useful test is your answer to the following question:

Does my team collaboratively and frequently perform well-documented repetitive processes?

If your processes are ad-hoc or you perform them only occasionally, it’s unlikely ServiceNow will provide value relative to its cost. ServiceNow is at root a process automation tool - repetition and standardization are central to its utility.

If the processes do not involve frequent hand-offs between employees to complete work, it’s unlikely ServiceNow is the best option. ServiceNow is likely too heavy-weight if individual employees are responsible for the lifetime of a work item. Consider a simpler task management or kanban board system if you're having issues with work tracking.

If your processes are not well documented, it's unlikely you can successfully steer the development of ServiceNow. You should consider doing this work first before seeking technology solutions.

What ServiceNow does well

ServiceNow is a premium tool - with substantial development and licensing costs. It can provide tremendous value if used to best effect but it will almost never be the cheapest or simplest solution.

If you would benefit from workflow automation, especially with processes that begin with a self-service form, ServiceNow might be right for you team.

If you have processes that require the coordination of many workers, each contributing some part of an overall deliverable, ServiceNow might ideal.

If you need granular metrics on task work - such as for Service Level Agreements or auditability, ServiceNow might be a good fit.

Fail Early

If these value drivers are not key to your team's success - it's unlikely ServiceNow will be worth the effort and expense.

It is important throughout the onboarding journey to bear in mind that ServiceNow may not be the right solution and discovering that fact is itself a valuable outcome of the onboarding process.
